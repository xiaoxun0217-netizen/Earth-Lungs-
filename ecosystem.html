Our Planet's Vital Ecosystems

Earth's ecosystems provide essential services that sustain all life. From regulating our climate to providing clean air and water, these complex systems are fundamental to our survival. At EarthLungs, we focus on protecting the most vulnerable and important terrestrial ecosystems worldwide.

Forest Ecosystems

Forests are the lungs of our planet, hosting incredible biodiversity and playing a crucial role in climate regulation. They cover approximately 31% of the world's land area and are home to more than 80% of terrestrial species.

31% of land area
80% of terrestrial species
1.6B people depend on forests
-10M hectares lost annually

Major Threats

  • Deforestation for agriculture and development
  • Illegal logging and unsustainable harvesting
  • Climate change impacts
  • Forest fires and pests

Wetland Ecosystems

Wetlands are among the most productive ecosystems on Earth, acting as natural water filters and flood controllers. They provide critical habitat for countless species and store significant amounts of carbon.

6% of Earth's surface
40% of species rely on wetlands
-35% lost since 1970
30% of land-based carbon

Major Threats

  • Drainage for agriculture and development
  • Pollution from agricultural runoff
  • Invasive species
  • Climate change and sea-level rise

Grassland Ecosystems

Grasslands cover vast areas of the Earth's surface and support unique biodiversity, including many grazing animals and grassland birds. They play a vital role in carbon sequestration and soil conservation.

40% of Earth's land area
11% of carbon stored in soils
-49% converted to agriculture
100+ grassland bird species at risk

Major Threats

  • Conversion to cropland
  • Overgrazing and degradation
  • Fragmentation from development
  • Loss of native species

Desert Ecosystems

Deserts may appear barren, but they host specially adapted life forms and play important roles in global climate patterns. These fragile ecosystems are highly sensitive to disturbance and climate change.

33% of Earth's land surface
2.1B people live in drylands
+10% desertification rate
500+ unique plant species

Major Threats

  • Desertification and land degradation
  • Overexploitation of water resources
  • Climate change intensifying aridity
  • Mining and resource extraction

Mountain Ecosystems

Mountains are water towers for the planet, providing freshwater to more than half of humanity. They harbor unique biodiversity and are particularly vulnerable to climate change impacts.

27% of Earth's land area
50% of global biodiversity hotspots
3.7B people depend on mountain water
-84% glacial ice loss by 2100 (projected)

Major Threats

  • Glacial melt and changing water availability
  • Habitat fragmentation
  • Unsustainable tourism and development
  • Climate change impacts on alpine species
